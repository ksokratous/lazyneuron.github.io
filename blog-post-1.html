F1 Visa Renewal and Other Struggles! Tips for International Students

Hello everyone and welcome to my first post!

My name is Konstantina Sokratous and I am beginning my 5th year of Ph.D. at the University of Florida. I am also expected to be an incoming Visiting Scholar at the OSU for the academic year 2024-2025. But that's all pending (!!!) and if wonder pending what, well the answer is US Immigration.

You broke the ocean in half to be here. Only to meet nothing that wants you...
- Immigrant

You might wonder why my first post on my academic/professional website involves a post that has very little to do with my skills or direct research. Well, in a way you're right, it has nothing to do with it but in another way, it has everything to do about it. To give you some context, I began to my current PhD program in August 2020. In my first letter of admission - or the initial offer if you prefer - came sometime after my interview in February and in that letter I received confirmation of my admission to the program. In that letter, it was detailed that the PhD program at the University of Florida is traditionally 5 years long. Funding was warrantied for 5 years but there is also a possibility for a 6th year of funding pending Departmental approval. Ok, so nothing new here, I was told during the interview anyway that if I wanted to do my PhD at UF then I needed to stay for 5 years instead of 4, even if I were to transfer either one of my master's degrees. One thus might think that it is only logical that the University will produce a document that would allow me (or allow the embassy) to get a VISA for 5 years, right? Rightttt? Well no! And I certainly cannot speak as to whether this lack of logic is universal to the US academic system or simply to the very problematic state of Florida, but at the end of the day they issued me a 4-year I-20, stating that the expected end of my program is summer of 2024, when they had in fact imposed the 5 year schedule themselves. Ok and so what you might think? You can get issued a new I-20 and then apply for a renewal. It is a seemless process they said. An administrative formality they said. Well surprise, surprise, whatever they said was pure bullshit.
