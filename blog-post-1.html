F1 Visa Renewal and Other Struggles! Tips for International Students

Hello everyone and welcome to my first post!

My name is Konstantina Sokratous and I am beginning my 5th year of Ph.D. at the University of Florida. I am also expected to be an incoming Visiting Scholar at the OSU for the academic year 2024-2025. But that's all pending (!!!) and if wonder pending what, well the answer is US Immigration.

You broke the ocean in half to be here. Only to meet nothing that wants you...
- Immigrant

You might wonder why my first post on my academic/professional website involves a post that has very little to do with my skills or direct research. Well, in a way you're right, it has nothing to do with it but in another way, it has everything to do about it. To offer some context, I began my PhD program in August 2020. In my first letter of admission, I received evidently confirmation of my admission to the program but also a confirmation that my funding was going to be warrantied for 5 years, as the PhD program at UF is traditionally 5 years long. Ok, so nothing new here, I was anyway told during the interview that if I wanted to do my PhD at UF, then I needed to stay for 5 years instead of 4, even if I were to transfer either one of my master's degrees.
One thus might think that it is only logical that the University will produce a document that would allow me to get (or allow the embassy to issue) a VISA for 5 years, right? Rightttt? Well no! And I certainly cannot speak as to whether this lack of logic is universal to the US academic system or simply to the very problematic state of Florida, but at the end of the day they issued me a 4-year I-20, stating that the expected end of my program is summer of 2024, when they had in fact imposed the 5 year schedule themselves. Ok and so what? You can get issued a new I-20 and then apply for a renewal. It is a seemless process they said. An administrative formality they said. Well surprise, surprise, whatever they said was pure bullshit.

July 22, 2024 Lazy Neuron
